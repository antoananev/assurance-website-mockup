What Happens to Your Roof After a Hail Storm?

Hailstones can range from marble-sized to softball-sized. Even smaller hail causes significant damage to shingles — cracking the granule coating, exposing the underlying asphalt, and creating entry points for water. This damage often can't be seen without getting on the roof.

Wind damage causes shingles to lift, crack, or go missing entirely. Flashing around chimneys and vents gets bent or separated. These gaps allow water to penetrate your roof decking and eventually your home's interior.

Signs Your Roof Has Storm Damage

  • Dents or dings on metal components (gutters, vents, AC units)
  • Granule loss — check your downspouts after a storm
  • Cracked, missing, or lifted shingles
  • Water stains on interior ceilings or walls
  • Damaged or bent gutters
  • Neighbors getting new roofs — a sign of neighborhood hail

Why You Need to Act Quickly

Insurance claims have deadlines — typically 1-2 years from the storm event. Waiting too long means missing your window. More importantly, small damage becomes big damage when left unaddressed — costing you thousands more in water damage repairs.

Why Metal Roofing Makes Sense in Texas

DFW gets some of the most severe weather in the country — hail, high winds, extreme summer heat. Metal roofing is built for exactly this environment. Unlike asphalt, metal reflects heat (reducing cooling costs), resists hail impact, and won't crack or curl in the Texas heat.

Benefits of Metal Roofing

  • 50+ year lifespan — often the last roof you'll ever need
  • Superior wind resistance — rated for 140+ mph winds
  • Energy savings — reflects solar heat, reducing AC costs up to 25%
  • Hail resistant — Class 4 impact rating available
  • Low maintenance — no shingles to crack, curl, or blow off
  • Increases home value
  • Environmentally friendly — often made from recycled materials

Metal Roofing & Insurance

Many insurance companies offer discounts for metal roofing due to its superior durability. If your current roof was damaged by hail, your insurance claim may cover an upgrade to metal — especially if your current shingles are older.

Styles We Install

  • Standing seam metal roofing — clean, modern look
  • Metal shingles — looks like traditional shingles, built like metal
  • Corrugated metal — cost-effective and durable
